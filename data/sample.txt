Industrial Pump Maintenance Manual

Section 1: Overview
This document describes the maintenance procedures for industrial centrifugal pumps used in manufacturing environments. Proper maintenance ensures reliability, efficiency, and reduced downtime.

Section 2: Common Failure Modes
1. Bearing wear due to insufficient lubrication.
2. Seal leakage caused by pressure imbalance.
3. Overheating due to improper cooling.
4. Cavitation resulting from incorrect suction pressure.
5. Vibration caused by shaft misalignment.

Section 3: Preventive Maintenance Schedule
Daily:
- Inspect pump for abnormal vibration.
- Monitor temperature levels.
- Check for unusual noise.

Weekly:
- Verify lubrication levels.
- Inspect seals and gaskets.
- Check alignment.

Monthly:
- Conduct vibration analysis.
- Perform thermal inspection.
- Review operating logs.

Section 4: Troubleshooting
Problem: Excessive vibration
Possible Causes:
- Misalignment
- Bearing damage
- Imbalanced impeller

Recommended Action:
- Check shaft alignment
- Inspect bearings
- Rebalance impeller

Problem: Overheating
Possible Causes:
- Blocked cooling system
- Low lubrication
- Excess load

Recommended Action:
- Clean cooling channels
- Refill lubrication
- Reduce operating load
